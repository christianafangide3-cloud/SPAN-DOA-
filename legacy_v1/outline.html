Official Course Syllabus

Faculty of Basic Medical Sciences • 200 Level

Department of Physiology

PHS 211: Introduction to Physiology & Blood

2 Units
  • Foundations: History of Physiology, Cell Membranes, Transport Processes, Biophysical Principles.
  • Homeostasis: Control Systems (Negative/Positive Feedback), Temperature Regulation, Biological Rhythms.
  • Blood Physiology: Haemopoiesis, WBC & Differential Count, Plasma Proteins, Coagulation & Fibrinolysis, Blood Groups (ABO/Rh), Transfusion hazards, Immunity & HIV.

PHS 212: Physiology of Excitable Tissues

2 Units
  • General Concepts: Structure & functions of Nerves, Cardiac muscle, Smooth muscle, and Skeletal muscle.
  • Nerve Physiology: Membrane Potentials, Genesis of resting membrane potential, Action potentials, Nerve impulse generation and conduction.
  • Synapses: Structure of synapse and neuromuscular junction; Transmission mechanisms.
  • Muscle Physiology: Theories of excitation-contraction coupling, classification of muscles.

PHS 213: Cell Physiology & Biophysics

2 Units
  • Cell Structure & Methods: Eukaryotic cell structure, Cell theory, Methods of studying cells (microscopic, extraction, culture).
  • Membrane Dynamics: Physical/chemical structure, Models of cell membrane, Passive & Active transport mechanisms, Vesicular transport, Membrane modifications in disease.
  • Internal Structure: Cytoskeleton, Desmosomes, Gap junctions, Tight junctions, Organelle functions.
  • Excitable Tissues Advanced: Intracellular vs Extracellular recording, Genesis of impulse.
  • Muscle Biophysics: Excitation, contraction and relaxation of Skeletal, Cardiac, and Smooth muscles. Physiology of smooth muscle excitation.

PHS 214: Intro to Laboratory Physiology I

1 Unit

Laboratory sessions on basic physiology experiments, specifically related to:

  • Frog sciatic nerve experiments.
  • Smooth muscle physiology.
  • Blood physiology experiments.

Department of Anatomy

ANA 211: Anatomy of Upper/Lower Limbs

3 Units
  • Upper Limb: Pectoral region, Breast, Axilla, Arm/Forearm compartments, Hand, Venous & Lymphatic drainage, Cutaneous innervation.
  • Lower Limb: Femoral triangle, Adductor canal, Gluteal region, Popliteal fossa, Leg compartments, Foot arches, Gait/Walking mechanism.
  • Clinical Anatomy: Nerve injuries, Applied anatomy of blood supply.

ANA 213: General & Systemic Embryology

2 Units
  • Gametogenesis & Cycles: Spermatogenesis, Oogenesis, Ovarian follicles, Ovulation, Corpus luteum, Menstruation, Uterine cycle & Hormonal control.
  • Early Development: Fertilization, Cleavage, Implantation, Gastrulation, Notochord, Neurulation, Folding of embryo.
  • Reproductive Technology: IVF, Surrogacy, Embryo transfer, Embryo manipulation, Twinning, Molecular embryology, Transgenesis.
  • Fetal Support: Fetal membranes, Placenta, Growth and Perinatology.
  • Systemic & Clinical: Limb development, Teratology, Congenital malformations, Cardiovascular system, Skin/Nails/Hair, Lymphoid organs, Glands (Endocrine/Exocrine).

Department of Biochemistry

MBC 211: General Biochemistry I

2 Units
  • Cell Biology: Cell theory, Organelles (Prokaryotes vs Eukaryotes), Chemical composition, Fractionation methods.
  • Water & Electrolytes: Total body water distribution, Regulation of water/electrolytes, Acid-Base balance, pH and pK values, Buffer systems.
  • Biomolecules: Chemistry of Carbohydrates, Lipids, and Nucleic acids.
  • Enzymology: Kinetics, inhibitors, classification.

General Studies

GST 212: Philosophy, Logic & Human Existence

2 Units
  • Philosophy: Scope, notions, meanings, branches and problems of philosophy.
  • Logic: Logic as a tool, Elements of syllogism, Symbolic logic, Rules of inference, Fallacies, Validity of arguments, Deduction/Induction.
  • Human Existence: Creative/Critical thinking, Philosophy and politics/religion/values, Character molding.
