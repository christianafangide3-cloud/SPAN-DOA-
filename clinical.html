The Student with Yellow Eyes

Patient Presentation

A 22-year-old male medical student presents to the clinic with extreme fatigue and dark urine (coca-cola colored).

History: He was treated for Malaria 3 days ago using an older antimalarial drug (Primaquine).
Observation: Examination reveals scleral icterus (yellowing of the eyes).

The Challenge

Based on your knowledge of Blood Physiology (PHS 211), what is the most likely diagnosis?

G6PD Deficiency

Glucose-6-Phosphate Dehydrogenase Deficiency

Physiological Mechanism:

1. The Defect: Lack of G6PD enzyme reduces NADPH production in RBCs.

2. The Trigger: Antimalarials (oxidative drugs) cause oxidative stress. Without NADPH, Glutathione cannot neutralize H2O2.

3. The Result: Hemoglobin precipitates (Heinz bodies), causing macrophages to bite them out ("Bite Cells"), leading to Acute Hemolytic Anemia.
